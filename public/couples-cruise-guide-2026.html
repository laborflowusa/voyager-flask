What's worth the upgrade — and what isn't

  • Yes
    Balcony or veranda stateroom — the single most impactful upgrade for couples. Waking up to open ocean with morning coffee on a private balcony is worth more than any ship amenity. Always choose balcony over interior savings.
  • Yes
    Thermal spa day pass (if not included) — most luxury lines include it; mid-range lines charge ~$40–80/day. Worth every dollar for couples who want quiet adult space away from the pool deck.
  • Yes
    One specialty dinner reservation — even on all-inclusive lines, signature restaurants often require a reservation. Book before you board. It's almost always the best meal of the trip.
  • Yes
    Princess Ultimate Balcony Dining — if sailing Princess, book this at least once. A private dinner on your balcony, fully set, with a personal server. Around $100/couple and one of the most romantic things you can do at sea.
  • Skip
    Shore excursion packages — bundled excursions sold onboard almost always cost more than booking locally at port. Research your ports in advance, book independently. You'll pay less and often have a better experience.
  • Skip
    Ship photography packages — quality is inconsistent, you're locked in before seeing the photos. A local photographer at any port will outperform the ship's team at a fraction of the cost.
  • Skip
    Drink packages on truly all-inclusive lines — on Regent or Viking, beverages are already included. On Virgin, the Bar Tab package ($300/couple for 5 nights) is worth buying in advance if you plan to drink regularly.

Frequently asked questions

What's the best adults-only couples cruise in 2026?
For pure adults-only experience, Virgin Voyages and Viking Ocean are the top two. Virgin wins on nightlife and dining variety. Viking wins on destination depth and intimacy. If budget is no object, Viking is the benchmark. If you want the most fun adults-only atmosphere at a more accessible price, Virgin Voyages is the answer.
Is a cruise actually romantic or just a tourist trap?
It depends entirely on which line you choose. Mainstream mega-ships with 5,000 passengers can feel more like a floating mall than a romantic escape. The lines in this guide — particularly Virgin, Viking, and Explora — were built specifically around the intimate couple experience. Choose right and a cruise is genuinely one of the most romantic travel formats available.
What's the best time of year for a Caribbean couples cruise?
November through April is the sweet spot — low humidity, lower storm risk, and the most pleasant weather. September and October are hurricane season and best avoided. May through August is peak season with higher crowds and prices, but the deals in May can be exceptional.
